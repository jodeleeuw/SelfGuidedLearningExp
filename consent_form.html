INDIANA UNIVERSITY INFORMED CONSENT STATEMENT FOR

Related and Unrelated Problems

You are invited to participate in a research study of the effectiveness of different study materials in the undergraduate courses P101 Introductory Psychology and P155 Introduction to Psychological and Brain Sciences. You were selected as a possible subject because you are a student in a participating section of P101 or P155. We ask that you read this form and ask any questions you may have before agreeing to be in the study.

The study is being conducted by Robert Goldstone and Benjamin Motz of the Department of Psychological and Brain Sciences.

STUDY PURPOSE

The purpose of this study is to measure the effectiveness of different variations (containing different degrees of related and unrelated problems) of study aids for helping students learn concepts related to descriptive statistics.

NUMBER OF PEOPLE TAKING PART IN THE STUDY:

If you agree to participate, you will be one of approximately 750 subjects who will be participating in this research.

PROCEDURES FOR THE STUDY:

If you agree to be in the study your score in this worksheet and in the exam will be analyzed as part of the present research study. All students will be submitting this homework quiz and taking the exam as part of normal class activities, but you may disallow the analysis of your responses by not agreeing to be part of this research. If you do not select the “I agree” button below, your grades will be excluded from analysis. Your instructor will not see the results of this quiz and will not know whether you agreed to have your scores analyzed or not.

RISKS OF TAKING PART IN THE STUDY:

As part of normal class activities, all students will be submitting homework, quizzes, and exams. If you consent to your responses being included in this study, the primary risk is loss of confidentiality (see “Confidentiality” below).

BENEFITS OF TAKING PART IN THE STUDY:

There will be no direct benefit to the participants in this study.

ALTERNATIVES TO TAKING PART IN THE STUDY:

An alternative to participating in the study is to choose not to allow your data to be analyzed. All students will be submitting homework, quizzes, and exams as part of normal class activities, but you may disallow the analysis of your responses by not signing this form. If you do not sign this form, your grades will be excluded from analysis.

CONFIDENTIALITY

Efforts will be made to keep your personal information confidential. We cannot guarantee absolute confidentiality. Your personal information may be disclosed if required by law. Your identity will be held in confidence in reports in which the study may be published.

Your instructor will not, at any time, have access to the information about whether you agreed to participate. You should feel comfortable knowing that you may choose to participate, or not participate, and your choice will be hidden from your instructor. If you agree to participate in this study by clicking the box “I agree’ bellow, only the researchers will have access to this choice.

Organizations that may inspect and/or copy your research records for quality assurance and data analysis include groups such as the study investigator and his/her research associates, the Indiana University Institutional Review Board or its designees, and (as allowed by law) state or federal agencies, specifically the Office for Human Research Protections (OHRP) who may need to access your research records.

COSTS

There are no additional costs associated with your consent to allow the analysis of your coursework.

PAYMENT

You will not receive payment for taking part in this study.

For questions about your rights as a research participant or to discuss problems, complaints or concerns about a research study, or to obtain information, or offer input, contact the IU Human Subjects Office at (812) 856-4242 or by email at irb@iu.edu

VOLUNTARY NATURE OF STUDY

Taking part in this study is voluntary. You may choose not to take part or may leave the study at any time. Leaving the study will not result in any penalty or loss of benefits to which you are entitled. Your decision whether or not to participate in this study will not affect your current or future relations with the researchers or the instructor.
